Tacos Arabes

Tacos arabes are a wonderful fusion of Mexican and Middle Eastern cuisine

Tacos árabes are, as the name suggests, "Arab" tacos, and they come from the city of Puebla in Mexico. It's hard to get a crystal-clear explanation of their origins, but the basic story is that Middle Eastern immigrants* arrived in Puebla sometime around or shortly after the First World War and brought with them the shawarma-making tradition of serving thinly sliced, spit-roasted marinated lamb in pita bread.

Here are the ingredients to make this wonderful dish.

  • 500g – 1 lb pork steak, sliced thinly in strips
  • 2 tablespoon lime juice
  • 2 teaspoon standard white vinegar
  • 1 large Spanish onion, sliced in thin wedges
  • A handful of finely chopped parsley
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme
  • 2 tablespoon oregano
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • Generous pinch of salt
  • 4 tablespoon cooking oil

Here are the steps

  1. Mix all the ingredients, except for the oil, in a bowl or re-sealable bag. Make sure the meat is covered evenly in the marinade and leave to rest in the fridge for at least 2 hours or even overnight if possible.

  2. To cook, first drain the excess liquid from your meat, then heat the oil in a big pan and fry gently until it cooks, will take about 15 minutes on medium heat.

  3. Serve with hot soft pita or flour tortillas.
